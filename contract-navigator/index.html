PROFESSIONAL SERVICES AGREEMENT

Between TechCorp Solutions LLC and Global Industries Inc.

1. PARTIES AND EFFECTIVE DATE. This Professional Services Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of January 15, 2025 by and between TechCorp Solutions LLC, a Delaware limited liability company ("Contractor"), and Global Industries Inc., a California corporation ("Client").

✓ GOOD: Clear party identification and effective date specified. Delaware LLC structure provides liability protection.

2. SCOPE OF SERVICES. Contractor shall provide digital transformation consulting services including system integration, process optimization, and technology advisory services as detailed in Exhibit A attached hereto and incorporated by reference.

✓ FAVORABLE: Well-defined scope with reference to detailed Exhibit A. Reduces scope creep risks.

3. COMPENSATION AND PAYMENT TERMS. In consideration for the Services, Client agrees to pay Contractor a total fee of $125,000 payable in monthly installments of $20,833. Payment shall be due ninety (90) days after receipt of invoice ("Net 90"). Late payments shall not accrue interest or penalties.

⚠️ HIGH RISK: 90-day payment terms are excessive (industry standard: 30 days). No late payment penalties. Major cash flow impact: ~$62K outstanding at any time.

RECOMMEND: Negotiate to Net 30 with 1.5% monthly late fees.

4. PERFORMANCE REQUIREMENTS. Contractor shall maintain dedicated on-site presence during Client's business hours (8 AM - 6 PM, Monday-Friday) and provide 24/7 emergency support response within 2 hours for critical system issues.

⚠️ MEDIUM RISK: 24/7 support requirement may exceed current staffing capacity. On-site presence limits flexibility.

ACTION: Verify staffing model can support these requirements before signing.

5. TERM AND TERMINATION. This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and continue for six (6) months unless terminated earlier. Either party may terminate for material breach with thirty (30) days written notice. Client may not terminate this Agreement for convenience.

⚠️ MEDIUM RISK: No termination-for-convenience clause locks Contractor into full 6-month commitment regardless of changed circumstances.

RECOMMEND: Add mutual termination-for-convenience with 30-day notice.

6. INDEMNIFICATION. Contractor agrees to indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Client from any and all claims, damages, losses, costs, and expenses (including attorney's fees) arising out of or relating to Contractor's performance of this Agreement, regardless of the cause of such claims or whether caused by Contractor's negligence.

🚨 CRITICAL: Extremely broad indemnification - covers ALL claims regardless of cause. Unlimited liability exposure could exceed contract value by 10x or more.

IMMEDIATE ACTION: 1) Limit to claims caused by Contractor's negligence 2) Add mutual indemnification 3) Cap liability at 12 months fees ($250K)

7. INSURANCE. Contractor shall maintain Commercial General Liability insurance of not less than $2,000,000 per occurrence, Professional Liability insurance of $1,000,000 per claim, and Cyber Liability insurance of $5,000,000 per incident.

📋 COMPLIANCE: Standard insurance requirements. Verify current policies meet these limits before signing.

ACTION: Obtain certificates of insurance from broker.

8. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY. All work product, deliverables, and intellectual property created by Contractor in performance of this Agreement shall remain the property of Contractor, with Client receiving a non-exclusive, royalty-free license for business use. Pre-existing intellectual property of each party shall remain their respective property.

✓ EXCELLENT: Strong IP protection for Contractor. Retains ownership of work product while granting Client necessary usage rights. Well-balanced approach.

9. CONFIDENTIALITY. Each party acknowledges that it may receive confidential information from the other party. Both parties agree to maintain such confidential information in strict confidence and not disclose to third parties without written consent.

✓ GOOD: Mutual confidentiality obligations protect both parties' sensitive information. Standard and appropriate terms.

10. GOVERNING LAW AND DISPUTE RESOLUTION. This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of Delaware. Any disputes shall be resolved through binding arbitration in Wilmington, Delaware under the Commercial Arbitration Rules of the American Arbitration Association.

📍 NEUTRAL: Delaware law is business-friendly. Arbitration clause may limit legal remedies but reduces litigation costs.

NOTE: Consider if Delaware venue is convenient for both parties.
